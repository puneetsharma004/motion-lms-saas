Add a single `layout` prop and Motion animates an element between *any* two
layout states — a flex-direction change, reordering, a size change — by measuring
its position before and after the change and tweening the difference. This is the
FLIP technique, and you get it for free. No manual coordinates.

<Instructions>

1. Open **Exercise.jsx** and import `motion` from `"motion/react"`.
2. Turn each of the three boxes into a `motion.div`.
3. Add the `layout` prop to each. That's the whole change.

</Instructions>

Press **Toggle Direction** to switch between row and column — the boxes glide to
their new positions instead of snapping.
