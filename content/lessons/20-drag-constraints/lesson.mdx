Plain `drag` lets an element go anywhere. Real draggables — sliders, sheets,
cards — stay inside bounds and *feel* physical. Point `dragConstraints` at a
container ref to box it in, `dragElastic` lets it stretch slightly past the edge
and snap back, and `dragTransition` gives it momentum so a flick coasts and
settles instead of stopping dead. `whileDrag` adds a grab affordance.

<Instructions>

1. Import `motion` from `"motion/react"` and keep the `constraintsRef`.
2. Add `drag` to the puck and bound it with `dragConstraints={constraintsRef}`.
3. Add feel: `dragElastic={0.2}` and `dragTransition={{ bounceStiffness: 400, bounceDamping: 18 }}`.
4. Add `whileDrag={{ scale: 1.1 }}` for grab feedback.

</Instructions>

Fling the puck — it coasts, bumps the walls, and springs back inside. That
physics is what makes drag UIs feel real.
