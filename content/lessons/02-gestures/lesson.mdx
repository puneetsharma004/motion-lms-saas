Motion turns interaction states into one-line props. Instead of wiring up event
handlers and state, you declare the animation for each gesture:

- `whileHover` — animate while the pointer is over the element
- `whileTap` — animate while pressed
- `drag` — make it draggable, with `dragConstraints` to keep it in bounds

<Instructions>

1. Open **Exercise.jsx** and import `motion` from `"motion/react"`.
2. Bind `ref={constraintsRef}` on the wrapper `<div>`.
3. Hover card → `<motion.div whileHover={{ scale: 1.1 }}>`.
4. Tap card → `<motion.div whileTap={{ scale: 0.9 }}>`.
5. Drag card → add `drag`, `dragConstraints={constraintsRef}`, `dragElastic={0.1}`,
   and `whileDrag={{ scale: 1.1 }}`.

</Instructions>

Hover, click, and drag the cards in the preview to feel each gesture.
