`animate` decides *what* changes; `transition` decides *how* it feels — and it's
the difference between "fine" and "delightful." Motion has two main types:

- **Tween** — time-based: `transition={{ duration: 0.4, ease: "easeInOut" }}`.
- **Spring** — physics-based: `transition={{ type: "spring", stiffness: 300, damping: 18 }}`. Higher `stiffness` = snappier; lower `damping` = more bounce. (You can also just set `bounce`.)

Springs feel natural because they carry momentum — which is why real product UIs lean on them.

2. Change its `transition` to `type: "spring"`.
3. Tune it with `stiffness` and `damping` (try `300` / `18`), or set a `bounce`.

</Instructions>

Play with the numbers — low damping overshoots and wobbles, high stiffness
snaps. This intuition carries into every animation you build.
