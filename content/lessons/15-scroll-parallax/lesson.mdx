The depth effect on modern marketing sites — where a background drifts slower
than the content in front of it — is parallax. The key is `useScroll` with a
**`target`** and **`offset`**: instead of whole-page progress, you get `0 → 1`
for *one element travelling through the viewport*. `offset: ["start end", "end start"]`
means "0 when the target's top hits the bottom of the view, 1 when its bottom
leaves the top." Map that with `useTransform` to move a layer.

<Instructions>

1. Import `motion`, `useScroll`, `useTransform` from `"motion/react"`.
2. Wire the card: `useScroll({ container: containerRef, target: targetRef, offset: ["start end", "end start"] })`.
3. Map progress to movement: `const y = useTransform(scrollYProgress, [0, 1], ["-30%", "30%"])`.
4. Make the background layer a `motion.div` with `style={{ y }}`.

</Instructions>

Scroll the card through the panel — the gradient drifts at its own pace behind
the text. That speed difference is the whole illusion.
